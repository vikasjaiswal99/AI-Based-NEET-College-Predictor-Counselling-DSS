💡 Tips & Application Guidance

📋 Apply on NSP Early

Most central government scholarships are processed through scholarships.gov.in. Create your NSP account as soon as you get your NEET result. The portal opens in September-October and closes by December. Late applications are NOT accepted.

📄 Keep Documents Ready

You'll need: Aadhaar card, bank passbook (in student's name with IFSC), caste certificate, income certificate, domicile certificate, Class 12 marksheet, NEET scorecard, college admission letter. Get these before the portal opens.

🔗 Apply to Multiple Schemes

You can apply for both central and state scholarships simultaneously. Many students miss state-level schemes. If you're from MP, check Vikramaditya, MMVY, Gaon Ki Beti, AND the NSP central schemes — you may qualify for more than one.

💰 Education Loans as Backup

All nationalized banks offer education loans for MBBS at 8-10% interest. Vidya Lakshmi Portal (vidyalakshmi.co.in) lets you apply to multiple banks at once. Loans up to ₹7.5 lakh require no collateral. Central government's interest subsidy scheme covers interest during moratorium for EWS students.

🏥 Government Colleges = Low Fees

Annual fees at government medical colleges range from ₹5,000 to ₹60,000/year — extremely affordable. AIIMS charges only ₹1,628/year. Focus on securing a government college seat through NEET, and your total 5.5-year MBBS cost can be under ₹3 lakh.
